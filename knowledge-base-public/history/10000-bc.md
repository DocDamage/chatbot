## Summary

10000 BC falls in the 10th millennium BC, roughly 10,000 BC to 9001 BC. Dates this far back are approximate and based on archaeological, geological, anthropological, and radiometric evidence rather than written records.

The period marks the beginning of the transition from the Paleolithic toward the Neolithic through Mesolithic and Epipaleolithic cultures. It sits near the start of the Holocene epoch, after the end of the last Ice Age.

## Events

- Around 10000 BC - The world was warming after the last Ice Age, and glaciers were retreating in many regions.
- Around 10000 BC - Many human groups still lived as hunter-gatherers across all continents except Antarctica and Zealandia.
- Around 10000 BC - The transition toward settled life and early food production began in some regions, especially around the Fertile Crescent.
- Around 10000 BC - Natufian communities in the Levant supported sedentary or semi-sedentary settlements before full agriculture.
- Around 10000 BC - Early Pre-Pottery Neolithic A communities emerged in the Near East.
- Around 10000 BC - Jericho/Tell es-Sultan shows evidence of early building activity in the broader 9600 BC to 8200 BC range.
- Around 10000 BC - Early cultivation of wild plants, including possible fig cultivation in the Jordan Valley, belongs to this broad transition period.
- Around 10000 BC - Saharan rock art and other African cultural evidence point to active prehistoric symbolic and subsistence systems.
- Around 10000 BC - The Clovis culture was present or emerging in North America in the broader late 10th millennium BC range.
- Around 10000 BC - Sites such as Gobekli Tepe, Hallan Cemi Tepesi, and Tell Qaramel may have been occupied during this millennium.
