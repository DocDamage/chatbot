## Canonical answer

The biggest pop-culture story of 1996 is hard to separate from music: Tupac Shakur was shot in Las Vegas on September 7 and died on September 13, 1996. It became a defining moment for hip-hop, celebrity media, and 1990s culture.

## Key facts

- Tupac Shakur's death became one of the defining music and celebrity stories of 1996.
- The Nintendo 64 launched and pushed 3D console gaming into a new era.
- *Independence Day* became the year-defining blockbuster movie.
- The Spice Girls broke through internationally with "Wannabe."
- Dolly the sheep was cloned, turning biotechnology into a mainstream news story.

## Context

1996 was a transition year for global media culture. Hip-hop was central to mainstream conversation, blockbuster movies grew louder and more event-driven, 3D gaming became more visible, and pop acts were increasingly marketed as global brands.
